Psychology (Pre-Dental)🧠

Hello, my name is Hannah Lee and I am a psychology major with aspirations of pursuing a career in dentistry, combining my passion for healthcare with a strong interest in understanding human behavior and patient connection. My academic background in psychology has provided me with valuable insight into communication, empathy, cognitive development, and the emotional aspects of healthcare, all of which I believe are essential qualities for becoming a compassionate and effective dental professional. Through my studies, I developed a deeper appreciation for the importance of patient-centered care and the impact that trust, understanding, and education can have on improving overall health outcomes.

As an aspiring dentist, I am especially interested in the connection between mental and physical health and how psychological factors can influence oral health habits, anxiety surrounding dental treatment, and patient well-being. I am motivated by the opportunity to help individuals feel more comfortable and confident in healthcare settings while promoting preventive care and healthy lifestyles. My long-term goal is to create positive patient experiences by combining scientific knowledge with empathy, professionalism, and strong interpersonal skills. Throughout my academic journey, I have demonstrated a commitment to personal growth, leadership, and lifelong learning. I value collaboration and enjoy working with others in environments that encourage curiosity, innovation, and meaningful impact. My coursework in psychology has strengthened my analytical thinking, research skills, and ability to communicate effectively with individuals from diverse backgrounds. These experiences have prepared me to adapt to challenging situations, remain detail-oriented, and approach problems with both critical thinking and compassion

In addition to my academic interests, I am eager to gain hands-on experience within the dental and healthcare fields through research involvement, shadowing opportunities, and clinical exposure. I am enthusiastic about learning directly from professionals and exploring different areas of dentistry to better understand patient care, treatment planning, and advancements in oral healthcare. I am particularly interested in opportunities that will allow me to contribute to research initiatives, strengthen my understanding of healthcare environments, and further develop the skills necessary for success in dental school and beyond. I am known for my strong work ethic, positive attitude, and genuine desire to help others. I approach every opportunity with curiosity, professionalism, and determination, striving to continuously improve both academically and personally. By integrating my background in psychology with my future goals in dentistry, I hope to make a meaningful impact in the lives of patients and contribute to a healthcare environment that prioritizes empathy, education, and accessible care. I am also in two dental clubs at University of California, Riverside, called the Pre-dental Society Club and Bright Smiles Medical Association. I was able to gain clinical experiences by shadowing dental students at the free clinic in Riverside. At the moment, I am shadowing a specialist in orthodontics. As I continue to pursue my goals, I remain open to opportunities that will challenge me, expand my experiences, and help shape my journey toward becoming a skilled and compassionate dental professional. I am excited to continue building my academic and professional experiences while connecting with mentors and professionals who can help guide and inspire me throughout my journey into the field of dentistry.
